    Welcome to Tony's personal website

    My name is Nuda “Tony” Zhang. I’m a senior undergraduate student at Cornell University, pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics.

    My interest lies in building distributed systems. As an undergrad researcher, I am currently working on designing an efficient Byzantine-fault-tolerant distributed transaction protocol. I’m also working on the Ovid framework, and thinking of how the concept of linearizability can be defined in a state machine replication context, particularly in interactions between replicated services.

    I spent Fall 2017 working on the compiler for the P4 network programming language with Professor Nate Foster.

    Next semester I will be a TA for CS 4820: Introduction to Algorithms.
    For the past three semesters I was a TA for CS2110: Object Oriented Programming and Data Structures at Cornell, where I’m responsible for leading weekly recitations comprising approx. 30 students.
